Using assumptive tones in your emails is turning your buyers off and killing your reply rates.

It's something I didn't even realize I was doing and makes sense when thinking it through as a buyer myself.

Will Allred and team have the data.

If you are using AI Agents, you should add an instruction to make sure that copy isn't assumptive like below:

A lot of the times in emails to prospects we use strong assumptive language like:

- I saw you're hiring. According to {blog}, X% of new sales hires don't hit quota.

- Noticed that while XYZ as an org has been increasing headcount overall, the engineering team has lost a few members in the past year

- It costs about $40k per head to hire an engineer and almost all heads of engineering I speak with are behind on hiring, and it's why retention is critical

The problem with this type of language is that you are a random person in a buyers inbox and the buyer is thinking... "Who the hell is this person, telling me about my business with no context?".

A better approach is to use softer or an "unsure" tone. Here's some examples:

- I saw you might be hiring. I could be wrong, but are you starting to think about ramp?

- I might be off here, but it looks like although your org has been increasing headcount overall, the engineering team has lost a few members in the past year

- This might not be the case for you, but it typically costs about $40k per head to hire an engineer and it's common for most heads of engineering to be behind on hiring

Words like "might", "typically", "it's common" soften the email and gets more replies.
