Hi. I'm Leslie and I'm here to admit that I am team - check a bag.

IF the trip is 2 nights, maybe I can stretch to 3 nights I may make an exception because I get it - arriving home and not having to wait at the baggage carousel where people - for reason's I cannot explain - stand with their shins against the carousel belt waiting for their bag that is practically still on the plane.

BUT I need my comfy cozy things!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

Here are my reasons and you can fight me in the comments -

1. The heckin' liquid rule still PLAGUES me

This is especially true if I'm packing for a speaking gig because all of my make-up is cream/liquid + I want my setting spray which is a mist.

Believe me when I tell you I've tried all of the little bottles and cut tins and this and that to make packing liquids a breeze - THEY ARE ALL A LIE.

2. Packing to carry-on is WAY more stressful.

I have to be so thoughtful about what I am going to pack instead of being able to just throw some options in a bag.

You may have guessed that I do not have a capsule wardrobe and I do not want one.

3. I already have WILD travel anxiety, carrying on makes it worse.

I just don't need to add another WHAT IF to my travel day by wondering if there will be space for my bag, if I'll need to gate check my bag, etc.

Is it over-packing or is it just packing to make sure I have the things that make me comfy and happy during my travels??!!

Every time you do your rep's job for them, you delay the day they learn to do it themselves.

When you fix the rep's messaging instead of coaching them through it, the rep gets the meeting and you get the habit of doing the rep's job.

Six months later you are still sweating over the same two buttons.

The short-term fix and the long-term fix are not the same decision.

I STRUGGLED to hit the button on the left as an early sales manager.

It just felt easier and faster to do it myself.

But then I was doing everything myself and I'd trained my reps to come to me with every little issue and ask instead of trying to self-solve.

It was a downward doom spiral.

I had to acknowledge that the reason I was defaulting to the "just do it myself" mindset was that I felt good being the central cog in the wheel.

It made me feel important and valuable and needed.

What I realized as I matured as a leader is that the true mark of exceptional sales leadership is that your team is so enabled and empowered - they can do it without you.

I see this mistake all the time with outbound.
Reps pick one channel and stick to it.
Or they try everything and spread themselves thin.

Neither works.

If you're only using one channel, you're forcing buyers to meet you on your terms. That's the problem.

Use at least three.
Email. Phone. Social.

Then adjust based on where your buyers actually engage. Not trends. Not preference.

Buyer behavior > everything else.
